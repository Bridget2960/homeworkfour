Dictionary App

The first-ever dictionary did not give definitions. The earliest single-language dictionary produced in English was the “Table Alphabeticall.” Produced by a man named Robert Cawdrey in 1604, the Table Alphabeticall contained around 3,000 words, none of which were given definitions, but synonyms. The point of the book, according to Cawdrey, was to introduce complicated words, so readers could better understand scriptures and sermons.
